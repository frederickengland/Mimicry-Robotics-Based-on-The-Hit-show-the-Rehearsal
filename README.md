# Mimicry-Robotics-Based-on-The-Hit-show-the-Rehearsal
## Abstract
Nathan Fielder explored in Season 2 of The Rehearsal how rehearsing social interactions and environments may help autistic individuals become more comfortable in unfamiliar situations. Inspired by this concept, this project attempts to recreate interview-style environments using robotics instead of human actors.
## Introduction
This concept doesn't just stop at interviews I plan to use it with changing / escalating environments based on scores of what it is simulating.
## Background and inspiration
As a big fan of Nathan Fielder and his integration of robotics in his shows. E.g. Claw of shame. I wanted to pursue the idea of rehearsing real-life scenerios with robotic implementation starting with the most controlled being an interview and then working from there with situations with greater complexities

## Potential applications
Well it may be able to help those fearful of the multiple unknowns of the "real world" by giving them more control and knowledge / rehearsal that can help build confidence and decrease fearfulness by practicing those scenerios.
One such application could be in the job world, an interview is a lot of pressure, it's a scenerio that has the weight of a livlihood and lifestyle change. By practicing in a pressure free environment it then allows them to get better and have the confidence and calmness to approach the real interview.
Then for the workplace it self which is also a an environment with lots of different people and high stakes situations that may be out of peoples comfort zones when approaching, well this concept could also be built in a way that could simulate those environments and help those uncomfortable by this, gain comfort.
